PhD Aim

To develop an AI system for the prediction of spatiotemporal dynamics in the built environment using near real-time geospatial IoT sensor data.

  • because it is useful for a number of applications
  • urban planning - understanding how people move around the city with high accuracy allow cities to be built around people
  • traffic management - short term predictions allow for better traffic management such as rerouting or smart traffic lights
  • security - understanding how people move around the city allows for better security measures to be put in place and more effective emergency response

    • Problem Statement

    Urban mobility systems exhibit complex behaviour which makes them difficult to predict.

    Complex Systems Have Certain Characteristics

    • Non-linearity
    • Emergence
    • Adaptivity
    • Some others...

    Basically everything that makes a systems behaviour difficult to predict...

    An change in one part of the system can have a large effect on the system as a whole. More than the sum of its parts - the system has properties that the individual parts do not - the behaviour of a crowd is different to the behaviour of an individual. Just because a system behaves a certain way now does not mean it will behave the same way in the future - the system adapts to changes in the environment - for example a new road being built, or a global pandemic changing individual travel patterns.

    Short-Term Predictions Can Be Made For Complex Systems

    Nowcasting is the prediction of the present, the very near future, and the very recent past.

    With enough real-time data, we can predict the present and the future with a high degree of accuracy for certain complex systems such as meteorological systems.

    Data Assimilation Is Used To Generate Predictions

    Data assimilation is the process of combining observations of a system with a model of the system to produce an estimate of the current state of the system.

    last bit of technical information accurate predictions can be made, in part, thanks to data assimilation methods

    Data Assimilation Is Used To Generate Predictions

    For example, in weather forecasting, data assimilation is used to combine real-time weather observations with ensemble predictions from physics-based models (PBMs) to produce a short-term forecast.

    Data Assimilation Is Used To Generate Predictions

    Mobility systems cannot be predicted effectively using PBMs because they exists at a different level of complexity to meteorological systems. Agent-based models (ABMs) are able to capture the complex behaviour of mobility systems more effectively.
