Defining Grey Belt

Defining Grey Belt

Defining Grey Belt

Land within the green belt that:

  • Contains brownfield sites
  • Does not strongly contribute to:
    1. Prevention of unrestricted urban sprawl
    2. Prevention of neighboring towns merging into one another
    3. Preservation of the setting and special character of historic towns

Defining Grey Belt

Important Exclusions - Protected land under planning policy:

  • Flood zones
  • Conservation areas.
  • Sites of Special Scientific Interest

Development Requirements:

  • Have appropriate transport connections.
  • Meet demonstrable unmet development needs.
  • Not undermine the overall purposes of the remaining Green Belt.
  • Comply with the "Golden Rules":
    1. Provide affordable housing
    2. Improve infrastructure
    3. Improvements to green spaces

Challenges and Limitations

  • Resolution limitations for land use parcels (fields, gardens, etc.)
  • High computational costs for processing high-resolution imagery

Approach

  • Process specific grey belt polygons with Segment Anything Model
  • Focus on smaller image segments to optimise processing
  • Refine detection by segmenting objects within land use parcels
